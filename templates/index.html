How to use Strata

What is the Index tab?

The Index tab lets you run Boolean keyword searches across an entire production and export hit reports to send to opposing counsel or keep for your records.

Getting started

1
Create a production — type a name in the sidebar and click +. One production per case or production set.
2
Add files — click Files to pick individual documents, or Folder to import an entire folder. Supports PDF, DOCX, XLSX, CSV, EML, MSG, PPTX, TXT, and images.
3
Build Index — click ⚡ Build Index. Strata reads every document and builds a searchable index. This only needs to happen once per production.
4
Search — enter your search terms, one per row, and click Search.
5
Export — click Hit Report to generate an Excel or PDF report showing which documents and pages each term hit on.
Tip: Click any page tag (e.g. p14) in the results to open that document directly at that page.

What is the Production tab?

The Production tab is for deposition prep and document packet building. Search for specific terms, hand-pick responsive documents, and export them as a ZIP with a responsiveness log.

How to build a document packet

1
Select your production — choose an indexed production from the dropdown.
2
Enter search terms — witness names, email addresses, key dates, or phrases.
3
Review results — documents are grouped by term. Check the boxes next to the ones you want.
4
Export ZIP — get a ZIP with selected documents plus a responsiveness log.
Tip: Great for building witness-specific packets before depositions.

What is the Redactions tab?

The Redactions tab scans a production for documents containing redactions — black bars and PDF redaction annotations — and tells you exactly which pages they appear on.

How to run a scan

1
Select a production — choose from the dropdown. Does not need to be indexed first.
2
Start Scan — Strata scans every PDF and Word document for redaction markers.
3
Review findings — each document with redactions is listed with page numbers and type.
4
Export — export a findings list, full report, or ZIP of all redacted documents.

Boolean operators

termination AND severance — both words must appear
fired OR terminated — either word
contract NOT expired — first word without the second
termination AND NOT severance — explicit AND NOT

Phrases, wildcards & fuzzy

"wrongful termination" — exact phrase
terminat* — wildcard: terminate, termination, terminated
~termination — fuzzy, catches misspellings

Proximity search

"fired" W/10 "email" — fired within 10 words of email
"Smith" W/5 "contract" — Smith within 5 words of contract
Tip: Combine operators: "John Smith" AND (fired OR terminated) AND NOT severance
