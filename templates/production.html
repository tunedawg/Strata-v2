How to use Strata

What is the Index tab?

The Index tab lets you run Boolean keyword searches across an entire production and export hit reports to send to opposing counsel or keep for your records.

Getting started

1
Create a production — type a name in the sidebar and click +. One production per case or production set.
2
Add files — click Add Files to pick individual documents, or Add Folder to import an entire folder including subfolders. Supports PDF, DOCX, XLSX, CSV, EML, MSG, PPTX, TXT, and images.
3
Build Index — click ⚡ Build Index. Strata reads every document and builds a searchable index. This only needs to happen once per production.
4
Search — enter your search terms, one per row, and click Search. Each term is searched independently.
5
Export — click Hit Report to generate an Excel or PDF report showing exactly which documents and pages each term hit on.
Tip: Click any page tag (e.g. p14) in the results to open that document directly at that page.

What is the Production tab?

The Production tab is for deposition prep and document packet building. Search for specific terms — witness names, email addresses, key events — then hand-pick the documents you want and export them as a ZIP with a responsiveness log.

How to build a document packet

1
Select your production — choose an indexed production from the dropdown. It auto-selects if you already have one loaded.
2
Enter search terms — use witness names, email addresses, key dates, or case-specific phrases. Add multiple terms to find documents responsive to any of them.
3
Review results — documents are grouped by term. Check the boxes next to the ones you want to include in your packet.
4
Export ZIP — click Export Selected. You get a ZIP containing the selected documents plus a responsiveness log showing which terms each document hit on and which pages.
Tip: Great for building witness-specific packets before depositions — search the witness's name, their email, and any key topics, then export everything responsive in one click.

What is the Redactions tab?

The Redactions tab scans a production for documents containing redactions — black bars and electronic PDF redaction annotations — and tells you exactly which pages they appear on. Useful for identifying what opposing counsel has withheld and flagging anything worth challenging.

How to run a scan

1
Select a production — choose from the dropdown. The production does not need to be indexed first.
2
Start Scan — Strata scans every PDF and Word document in the production for redaction markers.
3
Review findings — each document with redactions is listed with the page numbers and type of redaction detected.
4
Export — export a findings list as Excel, a full report as PDF, or a ZIP of all redacted documents.
Note: Strata detects black redaction bars and official PDF /Redact annotations. It does not detect white-out or correction fluid on scanned documents.

Boolean operators

termination AND severance — both words must appear
fired OR terminated — either word
contract NOT expired — first word without the second
termination AND NOT severance — explicit AND NOT

Phrases, wildcards & fuzzy

"wrongful termination" — exact phrase match
terminat* — wildcard, matches terminate, termination, terminated
~termination — fuzzy match, catches misspellings

Proximity search

"fired" W/10 "email" — fired within 10 words of email
"Smith" W/5 "contract" — Smith within 5 words of contract
Tip: You can combine operators: "John Smith" AND (fired OR terminated) AND NOT severance
